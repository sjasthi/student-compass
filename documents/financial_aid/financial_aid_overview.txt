Financial Aid Overview

Metro State offers several types of financial support to help students pay for their education. The university emphasizes affordability and provides guidance throughout the financial aid application process.

Types of Financial Aid
- Loans: Borrowed funds that must be repaid after graduation or when enrollment drops below half-time.
- Grants: Need-based aid that does not need to be repaid.
- Scholarships: Merit-based or need-based awards that do not require repayment.
- Student Jobs: On-campus or approved off-campus employment opportunities that help students earn money to support educational expenses.

Support for Students
Metro State assists students with:
- Understanding financial aid options
- Completing the financial aid application process
- Identifying available grants and scholarships
- Finding student employment opportunities

Additional Notes
- Financial aid can significantly reduce the cost of tuition.
- Student employment may be part of a financial aid package.
- Scholarship programs may have separate eligibility requirements.
