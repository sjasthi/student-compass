Senior Citizen Tuition (Age 62+)

Minnesota residents who are 62 years of age or older may enroll in courses at Metro State at a reduced cost. Enrollment is based on space availability.

Eligibility
- Must be a legal Minnesota resident.
- Must be 62 years old before the start of the term.
- Individuals receiving a railroad retirement annuity qualify at age 60.
- Eligibility is defined under Minnesota Statute § 135A.51.

Enrollment Requirements
- To audit courses, students must complete a visiting student application.
- To pursue a degree, students must complete a degree-seeking application for their chosen program.
- Students using the 62+ rate register on the first day of the term.
- Eligibility must be verified by showing a driver’s license to Gateway Student Services (in person or by email).

Costs for Credit Courses (Per Credit)
- Administrative Fee: $20
- Parking Fee: $13
- MSUSA Fee: $0.80
- Health Services Fee: $2
- Technology Fee: $14
- Student Center Fee: $8.50
- Personal Property and Service Charge: varies by course
Total Cost: $58.30 per credit (plus any applicable course-specific charges)

Costs for Audited Courses (Per Credit)
- Parking Fee: $13
- Health Services Fee: $2
- Personal Property and Service Charge: varies by course
Total Cost: $15.00 per credit (plus any applicable course-specific charges)

Additional Notes
- Senior citizens may take courses for credit or audit.
- Course availability is not guaranteed; enrollment depends on open seats.
- See Minnesota State Procedure 5.11.1.3 for full program details and qualifications.
